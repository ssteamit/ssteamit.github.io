The National Museum

The Museum of Civilization is the first unique museum of its kind in Egypt and the Arab world. The museum presents an integrated picture of the Egyptian civilization from prehistoric times to the modern era, emphasizing the concepts of continuity, constancy and stability in the Egyptian civilization through its material and intangible cultural heritage. The museum is distinguished by the display of unique archaeological collections, perhaps the most important of which are the royal mummies, and its new interactive display using the latest technological technologies. To reveal to us many of the secrets of the royal mummies, as well as shed light on the beliefs and rituals of mummification of the ancient Egyptian.

Islamic Art Museum

The Museum of Islamic Art in Cairo, Egypt is considered one of the greatest museums in the world, with its exceptional collection of rare woodwork and plaster artefacts, as well as metal, ceramic, glass, crystal, and textile objects of all periods, from all over the Islamic world.

Royal Jewelery Museum

The Royal Jewelry Museum is an art and history museum in the Zizenia neighborhood of Alexandria, Egypt. It is located in the former palace of Princess Fatma Al-Zahra'. The building's halls contain an inestimable collection of jewels and jewelry of the Muhammad Ali Dynasty.

Mummification Museum

The Mummification Museum is an archaeological museum in Luxor, Upper Egypt. It is dedicated to the art of Ancient Egyptian mummification.
